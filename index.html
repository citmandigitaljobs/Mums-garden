Eltham weather

Moon phase planting

New moon. Wait until a sliver of moon is visible, then plant, graft or transplant leafy plants. Do some pruning and fertilise.

First quarter (waxing) moon. Plant, graft or transplant fruiting plants (like tomatoes, capsicum, beans, peas), and fertilise plants.
Full moon. Plant bulbs and root crops. Prune and harvest. Avoid planging leafy plants.
Last quarter (waning) moon. Weed, cultivate, fertilise . Control pests. Prepare for new planting.

How do I...

Get rid of snails
Snail traps Making the ground uncomfortable for snails
Get rid of whitefly

Spray with a mixture of 500 ml water, half teaspoon biodegradable detergent, half teaspoon olive oil. Remember to check under leaves!

Stop animals eating my food
Rats in the compost More about rats in the compost How to use bird netting Metal-wrapping trees (might work for garden beds too!)
Get rid of powdery mildew
Grow plants in a sunny, airy position and avoid overfertilising. Remove and dispose of affected leaves. Try these home-made recipes Bi-carb soda spray
Get rid of scale

Spray with a mixture of 500 ml water and half a teaspoon of biodegradable detergent. Remember to check stalks, stems and under leaves!

Get rid of caterpillars

For orchids, cucumber, zucchini or hairy-leaf plants, spray caterpillars with whitefly spray. For other plants, use whitefly spray or a mixture of 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and half a tablespoon of biodegradable detergent in 500 ml of water
